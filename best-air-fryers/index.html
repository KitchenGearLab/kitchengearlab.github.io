How to choose

A short checklist that prevents buyer’s remorse.

Ignore marketing capacity first. Focus on daily workflow: how you load food, shake/flip, and clean. Most people regret friction, not features.

  • Usable capacity: what fits in one layer without crowding.
  • Workflow: if you often cook two items, dual-zone is a real upgrade.
  • Cleanup: fewer crevices, easier inserts, coating that rinses clean.
  • Controls: simple beats “smart” if you value speed and consistency.
  • Footprint: measure the space you actually have.
