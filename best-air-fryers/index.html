How to choose

A short checklist that prevents buyer’s remorse.

Ignore marketing capacity first. Focus on the daily workflow: how you load food, shake/flip, and clean. Most people regret friction, not features.

  • Usable capacity: what fits in one layer without crowding.
  • Controls: simple presets are fine, but manual control should be easy.
  • Cleanup: fewer crevices, easy-to-remove inserts, coating that rinses clean.
  • Results consistency: stability across real loads (not only “one portion”).
  • Footprint: measure your counter and storage space, not your optimism.
